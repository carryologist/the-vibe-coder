# Week One Complete: Building a Personal Blog from a Cabana in Cabo

And that's a wrap on week one! 

I set myself an interesting challenge: could I build a working personal blog in just five days, spending only 60-90 minutes each day, using nothing but my iPhone, all while lounging in a cabana in Cabo?

**The answer? Absolutely.**

## The Challenge Parameters

- **Timeline**: 5 days
- **Daily commitment**: 60-90 minutes
- **Device**: iPhone only
- **Location**: Cabana in Cabo (because why not mix work with paradise?)
- **Goal**: A functioning personal blog to catalog my journey through AI technologies

## Mission Accomplished

The blog is now live and ready to serve as my digital journal for exploring AI technologies. There's something incredibly satisfying about proving that you don't need a fancy setup or hours of uninterrupted coding time to ship something meaningful.

Working from my phone presented unique challenges – from navigating smaller screens to adapting my usual development workflow – but it also forced me to focus on the essentials and avoid the rabbit holes that desktop development often encourages.

## This Is Just the Beginning

While getting this blog up and running feels like a solid win, this is really just the foundation for what's coming next. I've got my eyes set on a much bigger project that I'll be diving into soon.

The blog will serve as my home base for documenting not just the technical aspects of working with AI, but also the process of building in public, the challenges of mobile-first development, and the lessons learned from unconventional coding setups.

## What's Next?

Stay tuned for that bigger project I mentioned – I'm excited to share more details soon. For now, I'm going to enjoy the fact that I successfully shipped a working blog while sipping drinks in paradise.

Sometimes the best coding sessions happen in the most unexpected places, with the most unexpected constraints. Here's to week two and whatever challenges it brings!
